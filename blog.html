APA citation format — the basics

APA format is the most commonly required style in college. The basic citation format for a website in APA is: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL

For a book in APA: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher.

MLA citation format — the basics

MLA format is common in humanities. The basic MLA citation format for a website is: Last, First. "Title of Page." Site Name, Day Month Year, URL.

For a book in MLA: Last, First. Title of Book. Publisher, Year.

Common citation mistakes to avoid

  • Missing the access date for websites in some styles
  • Wrong order of author name (first vs last name first)
  • Forgetting to italicize book and journal titles
  • Using the wrong citation style for your subject

How to Study Effectively: The Complete Guide

Studying effectively is the most valuable academic skill — and one of the least taught. Most students default to re-reading notes and highlighting, which research consistently shows are among the least effective methods.

The two most effective study methods

Active recall and spaced repetition are the top study methods according to learning science. Everything else is significantly less effective.

Active recall means testing yourself instead of passively reading. Close your notes and try to write down everything you remember. This forces your brain to retrieve information, which strengthens memory.

Spaced repetition means reviewing material at increasing intervals — today, in 3 days, in a week, in two weeks. Use our free flashcard maker to apply both methods automatically.
