Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between APA, MLA, and Chicago?
APA (American Psychological Association) is used mainly in social sciences and psychology. MLA (Modern Language Association) is common in humanities and literature. Chicago style is widely used in history. Your professor or assignment instructions will specify which one to use.
How do I cite a website in APA format?
APA website format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL. If no author, start with the title. If no date, write (n.d.). Example: Smith, J. (2023, March 15). How to study effectively. StudyTools. https://studytools.pro
How do I cite a book in MLA format?
MLA book format: Last, First. Title of Book. Publisher, Year. Example: Orwell, George. 1984. Secker and Warburg, 1949. For multiple authors, list them all in the order they appear on the title page.
Is this citation generator accurate?
This tool follows APA 7th edition, MLA 9th edition, and Chicago 17th edition guidelines. However, always review citations against your style guide or check with your professor — requirements can vary by institution.
What if some information is missing?
Leave blank fields empty — the generator will skip them. APA uses "n.d." for no date, and skips the author field if unknown. Always try to find missing information directly on the source before leaving it blank.
