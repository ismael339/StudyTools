APA Citation Examples for Students

APA citations look confusing until you learn the pattern. Most references answer the same questions: who made it, when was it published, what is it called and where can the reader find it?

APA website example

Format: Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page. Site Name. URL. If there is no named author, start with the title. If there is no date, use n.d.

APA book example

Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher. Book titles are italicised in formal APA formatting. Include edition information when it matters.

APA journal article example

Format: Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. Journal Name, volume(issue), page range. DOI or URL. The DOI is preferred when available.

Common mistakes

Students often capitalise every word in article titles, forget retrieval dates when needed, or mix APA with MLA punctuation. Use a citation generator to get the structure, then check assignment rules.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does n.d. mean in APA?

It means no date. Use it when a source has no clear publication date.

Do I need the access date in APA?

Usually no, unless the content is designed to change over time, such as a wiki or live database.
